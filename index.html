Youssef Alami Mejjati


Hi there I am Youssef Alami Mejjati, a final year PhD student in the university of Bath, UK. I am supervised by Dr Kwang In Kim, Dr James Tompkin and Prof Darren Cosker.

I am particularly interested in facilitating the image generation and editing pipelines for artists, professionals and practitioners. Currently, advanced image creation requires professional software, human expertise and a lot of time. As a consequence, the creativity of many individuals cannot be seamlessly expressed. My goal is to solve this through AI powered algorithms allowing users to express their ideas and imagination in a controllable manner with only few clicks.

During my PhD I have been blessed to work on many projects that pushed the progress towards automatic image editing. Using techniques, such as Generative adversarial networks (GANs) and Variational Auto-Encoders (VAEs), I created novel algorithms for Image-to-Image translation, Object insertion and synthesis on existing scenes, and Human Attention Re-direction.

Before starting my PhD I was studying in France, where I did my undergraduate in applied mathematics and statistics in Telecom SudParis (Institut Polytechnique de Paris), one of the leading engineering schools in France.

My hobbies include: Hiking, Travelling, Geography, Running, Climbing, Tennis.
